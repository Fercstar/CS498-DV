Data

The data used in the visualizations was taken from the Crime Data dataset at data.seattle.gov. It was filtered to show only data from 2008 - 2017 and pre-aggregated using Tableau Prep.

Visualization Structure

I used the martini glass hybrid structure. By scrolling through several pre-defined views, the user learns how to use the visualization works and is presented with some sample conclusions to help them analyze the view further.

Scenes

There are six scenes in the visualization. Most of the scenes show the user how the visualization works. In the last scene, the user can select a category of crime or an hour in the day to see either the crimes committed in that hour or the times when particular categories of crime are committed.

Annotations

There are a few annotations throughout the visualization. The first one alerts the viewer to the spikes in the histogram at 0 and 12 that are likely caused by the time of occurance being estimated. The second and third annotations point show how different crimes are more likely to occur at different times of the day. Throughout the visualization the view can hover their mouse over the bars to get additional details on demand in the form of a tooltip.

Parameters

There are several parameters the user can interact with to control the state of the visualization. In the beginning slides, the only thing the user can do is move the visualization backward and forwards by using the arrows to the right and left of the charts. They can also view tooltips at all times.

On the last scene, the user can change the parameters of the visualization by clicking on any of the bars in the Hour Crime Occurred and Category of Crime bar charts.

Triggers

The parameters in the visualzations drive several triggers. When a user moves forward or backward through the scenes of the visualization, the progress bar moves with the user to show them where they are at. Additionaly, there are triggers to change bar colors based on user selections and scene changes.

Limitations

The visualization is best viewed on a computer and not a mobile device.

Design Choices

Throughout the visualization, I went with a minimalistic style. I deliberately did not include legends on the chart, because I wanted the viewer to be thinking about relative frequencies and not absolute frequencies (e.g. how DUIs are distributed throughout the day). The total number of crimes a bar represents can be viewed on demand through the tooltip.
